# Garahe
### Your garage. Your story. Privately yours.

> A private vehicle companion app. Not a social network. Not a marketplace. Not a forum. A personal relationship between you and every vehicle you own, dream about, or have ever worked on.

---

## The problem

Car culture right now is loud.

YouTube builds. TikTok stunts. Instagram flexes. Reddit arguments about which oil is better. The community is hyperconnected and, for a lot of enthusiasts, exhausting. Every platform is designed to make you perform your passion for an audience — to post your build, share your mods, rack up likes from strangers who will forget your car by tomorrow.

Somewhere in all of that noise, the person who just wants to *know* their vehicle — deeply, quietly, personally — has no tool built for them.

And outside the enthusiast world, the problem is just as real. An everyday owner takes their car to a new mechanic and has to start from scratch. A secondhand buyer is handed a logbook that could be forged. A manufacturer ships a vehicle and loses the customer relationship the moment it leaves the showroom. Nobody is connecting these people in a way that actually serves them.

Garahe is built to fix all of that — without adding more noise.

---

## What Garahe is

Garahe is a private, offline-first vehicle companion with four layers:

- **The Garage** — your real vehicles, fully documented
- **The Workshop Log** — your growing personal knowledge base
- **The Dream Lot** — the vehicles you want, compared honestly to what you have
- **The Trust Layer** — a controlled way to share your history with mechanics, buyers, and manufacturers

There are no social features. No feed. No followers. No sharing mechanic. No ads. No algorithm recommending trending mods. The only thing Garahe grows is your relationship with your machine.

---

## Who it's for

Garahe is designed for four distinct users — and built so that each one makes the experience better for the others.

---

### 1. The vehicle owner

**The scenario:** Ryu owns a Honda Civic and a Kawasaki Z650. He's meticulous about maintenance but keeps notes scattered across his phone, a notebook in his glovebox, and his memory. When something goes wrong, he can't always remember what was done, when, or with which parts.

**What Garahe gives him:**

Ryu opens Garahe every time he does anything to a vehicle — even small things. Checked the tire pressure? Logged. Changed the air filter himself? Logged with notes, cost, and a photo of the part. The app tracks his actual odometer and reminds him when the Z650 oil change is coming — not based on a calendar, but based on how many kilometers he's actually driven since the last one.

Over time, his Workshop Log becomes something more than a record. It becomes the most honest and complete manual of those specific vehicles that exists anywhere — written by the person who knows them best.

**Key features:**
- Multi-vehicle garage (cars, motorcycles, trucks — any vehicle)
- Odometer-based and date-based service reminders
- Parts tracker — brand, cost, source, date installed
- Problem diary — log symptoms, diagnoses, and how you solved them
- Photo and receipt attachment per entry
- Vehicle health score — a single honest rating of current condition
- Workshop log — a private, chronological record of everything learned and done

---

### 2. The secondhand buyer

**The scenario:** Kenji wants to buy a used Kawasaki Z900. He's shown a physical logbook that could be anything, told "it's been well maintained," and has no independent way to verify a single claim. He either trusts blindly or walks away.

**What Garahe gives him:**

When a seller has maintained their vehicle on Garahe, they can generate a verified, tamper-proof history report — every service logged, every part changed, every problem recorded and how it was resolved. The buyer doesn't just see what was done. They see the *pattern* of care. Was it maintained consistently, or let go until something broke? Did the owner notice a warning sign and ignore it for months?

When the sale is completed, the full Garahe history transfers to the new owner. Kenji doesn't start with a blank record — he inherits the previous owner's complete log and continues building on it.

**What a blank record means:** If a seller has no Garahe history, the buyer knows immediately that the vehicle's care is unverified. Not a dealbreaker — but a negotiating reality. The absence of data is itself data.

**Secondhand features:**
- Shareable verified history report (PDF or in-app link)
- Vehicle health score visible to buyer
- Full service timeline with timestamps and odometer readings
- Ownership transfer — history moves with the vehicle, not the person
- Seller can choose what to share and what to keep private

---

### 3. The owner at a new service center

**The scenario:** Mia moves cities and takes her Toyota GR86 to a mechanic she's never met. They know nothing about the car. They run standard checks, recommend things she had done six months ago, and charge her for redundant work. She can't prove what's been done. They have no way to know the vehicle's real history.

**What Garahe gives her:**

Mia taps "Share with service center" and generates a one-time access code. The mechanic enters it — no account needed, no app required on their end. They immediately see her complete vehicle history: every part changed, every service done, every known issue and its resolution.

They skip the redundant checks and go straight to what actually needs attention. After the session, the mechanic can add their own service entry to Mia's record — with her approval. Her history grows richer every time someone works on the car.

**Service center features:**
- One-time access code — no mechanic account needed
- Full history visible: parts, services, known issues
- Personal data never shared — vehicle data only
- Access expires after the session automatically
- Mechanic can append their service entry with owner approval
- Verified service badge for authorized service centers

---

### 4. The dreamer

**The scenario:** Someone has a Kawasaki Z650 but spends their evenings on YouTube watching Z900 RS reviews. They know they want it. They don't know if they can afford to own it, maintain it, or whether it's actually better suited to them than what they have.

**What Garahe gives them:**

The Dream Lot is a wishlist treated seriously. When a vehicle is added, Garahe doesn't show an ad. It shows an honest comparison — built on real owner-reported data, not manufacturer specs or sponsored content.

**The Dream Lot shows:**
- Estimated monthly ownership cost vs. your current vehicle
- Common real-world issues reported by owners at various mileage points
- Your realistic timeline to purchase at your current financial position
- Estimated resale value of your current vehicle using its Garahe health score
- Upgrade complexity — how different the new vehicle is to maintain vs. what you know
- Side-by-side specs: power, weight, fuel economy, typical service intervals

**What makes this different from any other wishlist:** The comparison is built on honesty. Real costs. Real problems. Real timelines. No dealership referral links. No financing calculators designed to make a purchase feel more affordable than it is. Just the truth about what it would actually mean to own that vehicle.

## The Workshop Log — your private knowledge base

This is the feature that separates Garahe from every maintenance app on the market.

Every fix, every upgrade, every modification, every problem solved gets logged in the Workshop Log. Not as a data entry. As a personal record — what happened, what you tried, what worked, what didn't, what you learned.

Over years of ownership, the Workshop Log becomes something genuinely valuable: a complete picture of your skills and knowledge as an enthusiast, built from real experience with real machines. It belongs entirely to you. It doesn't feed an algorithm. It doesn't get analyzed to show you ads. It doesn't get shared without your explicit action.

When you sell a vehicle, the Workshop Log entries for that vehicle transfer with it — giving the next owner a living manual written by the person who knew the machine best.

## The business angle — why manufacturers care

Garahe isn't just a consumer app. It has a direct value proposition for the manufacturers whose vehicles are in it.

**The problem manufacturers have today:** They lose the customer relationship the moment a vehicle leaves the showroom. They have no idea what happens to it after delivery unless it comes in for a warranty claim. Their data comes from connected sensors in new vehicles — useless for older models, and structured around what the car reports, not what the owner actually experiences.

**What Garahe offers them:**

Users can opt in to share anonymous aggregate data with their vehicle's manufacturer. Not personal data. Not location. Aggregate real-world truth:

- How often is the Z900 chain adjusted in actual use vs. the recommended schedule?
- What's the real failure pattern of the GR86 clutch at high mileage?
- Which parts do enthusiasts replace first, and with what aftermarket alternatives?
- What problems are owners logging that never make it to a dealer?

This is worth more to an R&D team than any focus group or warranty dataset — because it comes from the people who actually maintain their vehicles, not just the ones who bring them to dealerships.

**The authorized service tier:** Garahe offers a verified partner badge for manufacturer-authorized service centers. When a customer shares their Garahe history with an authorized mechanic, the manufacturer gets to close the loop on what happens to their vehicles post-sale. The mechanic gets richer context. The customer gets better service. The manufacturer gets real-world data. All three win.

**Recall intelligence:** If 300 Kawasaki Z650 owners all log the same chain issue appearing around 30,000 km, Kawasaki knows before a recall becomes necessary. Proactive. Private. Built on real data from real riders who chose to share it.

## Competitive landscape — what exists and how Garahe is different

Several apps already do parts of what Garahe does. Here is an honest account of the market.

**What already exists:**
- ServiceLog, GarageLog, MyAutoLog, and VMT all offer maintenance logging, service reminders, and PDF export for buyers. Some are free. Some are well-designed.
- Loggy allows ownership transfer via email when a vehicle is sold.
- CARFAX pulls service history by VIN and auto-updates records at partner shops — powerful, but US-centric and reliant on partner network.
- Toyota and other manufacturers already collect anonymous aggregate vehicle data from connected cars — but through their own systems, not opt-in platforms.

**What does not exist — Garahe's genuine differentiation:**

| Feature | Garahe | Existing apps |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Lot with honest owner-reported comparison | ✓ | ✗ |
| Vehicle health score tied to resale value | ✓ | ✗ |
| Identity layer — vehicle as personal story, not spreadsheet | ✓ | ✗ |
| One-time mechanic access code, no account needed | ✓ | ✗ |
| Workshop Log as personal knowledge base | ✓ | ✗ |
| Opt-in anonymous data to manufacturers | ✓ | ✗ |
| No social features, no ads, no algorithm — by design | ✓ | ✗ |
| Ownership history transfer on resale | Partial | Partial (Loggy) |
| Maintenance logging and reminders | ✓ | ✓ |
| PDF export for buyers | ✓ | ✓ |

**The honest risk:** The maintenance log layer is a crowded space. If Garahe ships only a maintenance tracker, it competes with free apps and loses. The differentiation lives in the Dream Lot, the Workshop Log, the identity framing, and the manufacturer data partnership. Those are what nobody else has built.

## Why this is disconnected by design

Every app built in the last decade has been designed to spread. Share this. Tag someone. Post your build. Refer a friend. The entire architecture of modern software optimizes for network growth — more users, more data, more engagement, more ads.

Garahe is built on the opposite principle. And that is not a missing feature. It is the product.

**What Garahe has no mechanism for:**
- No social feed — your garage is not a timeline
- No likes, comments, or followers — your build doesn't need an audience
- No sharing button — data is shared only when you choose, to whom you choose, for how long you decide
- No discovery algorithm — Garahe does not suggest vehicles or trends
- No referral incentives — growth comes from genuine value, not recruitment
- No ads — not from manufacturers, not from parts sellers, not from anyone

**What replaces all of that:**

A private record that gets more valuable the longer you use it. An honest comparison tool that respects your intelligence. A trust layer you control completely. Knowledge that compounds quietly over years of ownership.

In a market where every platform is trying to turn enthusiasts into content creators and customers into data sources, Garahe is built for the person who just wants to know their machine.

That person has always existed. Nobody has built for them yet.

## Why Japan — why now

Japan is not an arbitrary market choice. It is the natural home for this product.

The world's most respected vehicle manufacturers — Honda, Toyota, Kawasaki, Yamaha, Suzuki, Mazda — are Japanese. They built their reputations on engineering integrity, long-term reliability, and a philosophy of continuous quiet improvement. Their customers, both in Japan and globally, share those values.

Japanese culture already has words for what Garahe embodies:

**Kaizen** — continuous, quiet, incremental improvement. Not dramatic overhaul. Small, consistent gains over time. That is exactly what the Workshop Log is.

**Shokunin** — the craftsperson who perfects their work without an audience. The mechanic who adjusts the chain tension because it needs to be right, not because anyone is watching. Garahe is built for that person.

**Monozukuri** — the art of making things. A deep respect for the object itself, for the craft of creation and maintenance. Garahe treats a vehicle not as a depreciating asset but as something worth understanding completely.

Garahe does not introduce a foreign concept to Japanese enthusiasts. It gives a digital home to something they already believe.

## Business model

Garahe generates revenue without ads and without selling user data. Three clean streams:

**User subscriptions (primary)**
- Free tier: one vehicle, basic maintenance log, odometer reminders
- Pro tier ($4.99/month or $39/year): unlimited vehicles, Dream Lot comparisons, Workshop Log, full history export, one-time mechanic access codes
- The core relationship — between the user and their vehicle — is never paywalled

**Manufacturer data partnerships**
- Opt-in anonymous aggregate data licensed to manufacturers on a per-model, per-market basis
- Users see exactly what data is shared before opting in
- Priced per manufacturer per market — recurring annual contract
- This is the high-margin revenue stream as the user base grows

**Authorized service tier (B2B)**
- Service centers and dealerships pay for verified Garahe partner status
- Unlocks richer history access when customers share, and the ability to append verified service entries
- Priced per location per month — direct sales to manufacturer dealership networks

## What success looks like early

Garahe does not need to scale globally on day one. It needs to prove one thing first: do users who log their vehicles on Garahe maintain them better, sell them for more, and feel more confident at a new service center than users who don't?

If the answer is yes — and it will be — everything else follows.

**Early targets:**
- Launch in one market: Japan or the Philippines as a Southeast Asian gateway
- 1,000 active users, 3 vehicle manufacturers in conversation within 12 months
- One authorized service center partnership as proof of concept for the B2B tier
- Measure: Dream Lot conversion rate (users who planned a purchase and followed through), Workshop Log depth (average entries per vehicle per month), and health score accuracy vs. actual resale prices
